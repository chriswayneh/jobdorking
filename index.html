Frequently asked questions

What is a Google dork?

A Google dork is a search built with Google's advanced operators, like site:, intitle:, and after:, to find pages a normal keyword search misses. JobDorking chains together the operators job hunters use most, so you don't have to memorize the syntax.

Which job boards can JobDorking search?

LinkedIn, Indeed, Dice, Greenhouse, Lever, Ashby, Workday, and ZipRecruiter out of the box. Leave every source unchecked to search all of Google, unrestricted to any site.

Does JobDorking scrape or store job listings?

No. JobDorking only builds the search-engine query in your browser. It doesn't crawl job boards, store postings, or require an account, and results come straight from Google's own index.

Why do I see fewer results with the 24h filter?

Google's after: operator filters by the date a page was indexed, not necessarily when it was posted, so very recent listings can lag behind by a day or two. Widening the window to 3 days or 1 week usually surfaces them.

Is JobDorking free?

Yes. JobDorking is free to use, with no sign-up required to build or run your search query.
